Whose

Asks about possession or ownership

Asks about: owner / possessor

Structure

Whosenounauxsubjectverb?

📘 Vocab note

Don't confuse Whose (possessive) with Who's (who is) — sound the same, different meaning.

Present Simple

Question

Whose book is this?

Answer

It is Maria's book.

Tip: Whose always refers to something that belongs to someone. It needs a noun after it.

🔍

Fetching Info

Questions are the tools you use to fetch information. Each WH word fetches a different type: place, time, reason, person.

💬

Short Answers

Real English uses short answers — never repeat the full verb. 'Yes, I do.' not 'Yes, I like pizza.'

🚫

Negation Logic

NOT goes after the auxiliary, never before the main verb. The main verb never carries the negative.
